📚 Stock Investing Basics

What is a Stock?

A stock represents ownership in a company. Shareholders can benefit from company profits and asset growth.

Types of Stocks

Common stocks offer voting rights and dividends; preferred stocks pay fixed dividends and take priority during liquidation.

Growth vs. Value Investing

Growth stocks offer high earnings potential but more risk, while value stocks are undervalued with solid fundamentals.

Index Funds

Diversify instantly by investing in S&P 500, NASDAQ-100, or NSE20 indexes, which track major market segments.

💡 Smart Investing Tips

  • Start with diversified portfolios to manage risk.
  • Use dollar-cost averaging for consistent investing regardless of market highs or lows.
  • Keep long-term goals in focus and avoid panic selling during dips.
  • Use research tools to evaluate company earnings, trends, and P/E ratios.
  • Reinvest dividends to enhance compounding returns.
