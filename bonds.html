Bond Investment Plans

At Vanguard Wealth Markets, we offer a variety of bond investment opportunities tailored for both conservative and income-seeking investors. Bonds provide predictable income, capital preservation, and portfolio diversification.

Kenya Treasury Bonds

Backed by the Government of Kenya, these bonds are ideal for long-term investments with semi-annual fixed interest payments.

  • Tenure: 1 to 30 years
  • Tax-free for Kenyan residents
  • Minimum investment: Ksh 50,000

Corporate Bonds

Issued by top-tier corporations in Kenya and abroad, these bonds offer competitive yields and risk-adjusted returns.

  • High yield with moderate risk
  • Diversification from government securities
  • Backed by company revenue or assets

Green Bonds

Eco-conscious investment in environmental projects like renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, and clean transportation.

  • Backed by both private and public issuers
  • Support climate-friendly infrastructure
  • Attractive to ESG investors

Infrastructure Bonds

Tax-free investments supporting national development projects such as roads, energy, and water supply systems.

  • Tenure: 5 to 25 years
  • No withholding tax on interest income
  • Issued by the Kenyan government

Why Invest in Bonds?

Stable Income

Receive predictable interest payments, usually semi-annually or annually, regardless of market fluctuations.

Capital Preservation

Ideal for risk-averse investors, bonds help protect your principal while earning returns over time.

Diversification

Reduce portfolio risk by balancing stocks and real estate with fixed-income securities like bonds.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I buy bonds on Vanguard Wealth Markets?

You can invest directly via your account dashboard. We offer easy onboarding and secure transactions.

Are bonds safe?

Government bonds are considered very safe, while corporate and green bonds carry slightly more risk but higher potential returns.

Can I sell my bonds before maturity?

Yes, most bonds can be sold in the secondary market, though price and demand may vary depending on the bond type.
